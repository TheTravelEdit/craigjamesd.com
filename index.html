Sandy Hook,
New Jersey

Raised on the shores of Barnegat Bay, Davitt has spent his life within sight or sound of the water. Those early years — fishing for snappers with bobber rigs, crabbing on the family's old Dixie, watching light shift over the open bay — became the visual vocabulary he would later pour onto canvas.

Working in oil on canvas and colored pencil, his paintings place heavy emphasis on shadow, texture, and hyper-realism. He often trades his brush for a palette knife, loading thick paint that dries with a sculptural surface — his subjects look like you could reach out and pick them up.

His work is held in private collections across the Northeast and shown in galleries throughout the Jersey Shore. In 2026 he was named the featured Angling Artist in On The Water Magazine's annual Angler's Almanac.

"I just paint the things I like because they have meaning to me — and if people find meaning in them too, that's great."
— CJ Davitt · On The Water Magazine, 2026
